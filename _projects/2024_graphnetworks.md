This study explores the fundamental concepts and applications of graph networks, with a particular focus on **spectral graph theory**. By analyzing graph structures through matrices such as the Laplacian and adjacency matrices, the study leverages eigenvalues and eigenvectors to capture the intrinsic properties of complex networks. These spectral representations enable a deeper understanding of node relationships and global connectivity patterns. Building upon this theoretical foundation, the study further examines how graph-based representations can be integrated into neural network architectures, illustrating the transition from traditional graph analysis to modern graph neural networks (GNNs). Overall, this work highlights the importance of spectral methods in bridging mathematical graph theory and practical machine learning applications for modeling structured data.
