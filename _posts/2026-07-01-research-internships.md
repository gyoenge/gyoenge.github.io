## Overview

I participated in an **undergraduate research internship at GIST** from 2025 to 2026. During this period, I conducted research in Medical AI and Computer Vision across the **[AI-MED](https://www.aimed-lab.com/)** (AI on Medical Applications) Lab and the **DMCB** (Data Mining and Computational Biology) Lab.

My primary research focus was on **predicting spatial transcriptomics from pathology images**. In simple terms, this task involves estimating the spatial distribution of gene expression levels of cells directly from histopathology images.

Through this experience, I gained hands-on expertise in deep learning, data analysis, and interdisciplinary research.
In particular, I studied a wide range of deep learning paradigms, including Convolutional and Transformer-based vision encoders, genetic language models, foundation models, self-supervised learning, multimodal representation learning, and contrastive learning.
By actively participating in weekly lab meetings and iteratively developing research ideas, I strengthened my ability to think critically, tackle complex problems with persistence, and effectively communicate technical concepts with others.

Overall, this period marked a **significant phase of both academic and personal growth**.

## GIST AI-MED Lab

- Duration: Sep 2025 - Present (Sep-Dec: online, Jan-Present: offline)

- Conducted research on Medical AI, focusing on computational pathology and multimodal representation learning.

- Developed **RaPaCL-ST (RadiomicsFeature-Pathomics Contrastive Learning)**, a multimodal framework for spatial transcriptomics prediction.
- Designed a method to bridge **handcrafted radiomics features** and **deep pathomics features** extracted from histopathology images.

- Utilized contrastive learning to align radiomics-based interpretable signals (e.g., texture, heterogeneity) with high-dimensional image embeddings in a shared latent space.
- Aimed to improve the biological relevance and interpretability of learned representations for downstream tasks such as spatial gene expression prediction.

- Gained experience in model design, multimodal learning, and research-oriented problem solving.

## GIST DMCB Lab

- Duration: Mar 2025 – Aug 2025 (Mar–Jun: onsite, Jul–Aug: hybrid)

- Participated in the G-SURF (GIST Summer Undergraduate Research Fellowship) program.
- Conducted a research project on extending foundation models for single-cell epigenomics to neurodegenerative diseases.

- Proposed an approach to extend **EpiAgent**, an in-silico perturbation framework, from cancer to Alzheimer’s disease (AD).
- Designed a method incorporating **cell-type specific LoRA (Low-Rank Adapters)** to model brain cell heterogeneity.

- Applied the framework to single-cell ATAC-seq datasets from AD and control brain tissues.
- Improved the ability to simulate regulatory responses across major brain cell types, including neurons, astrocytes, microglia, and oligodendrocytes.

- Gained experience in applying foundation models, handling high-dimensional biological data, and designing model extensions for domain-specific problems.
